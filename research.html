Evolution and fitness of S. cerevisiae in fluctuating environments

I'm studying the evolution of DNA-barcoded S. cerevisiae to fluctuating environments in the Petrov Lab, particularly the evolution to directional transitions in three-environment cycles (in collaboration with Dr. Clare Abreu).

Resource Allocation in Fluctuating Environments

Using models and experimental techniques, I am studying how microbes allocate resources in fluctuating conditions, particularly in the context of the carbon hierarchy.

Counting the Number of Labelled Histories in Galled Trees

In my rotation in the Rosenberg lab we generalized an algorithm to count the labeleld histories (a useful mathematical property of phylogenetic networks) for phylogenetic trees to galled trees, a class of phylogenetic networks. Our paper "All galls are divided into three or more parts: recursive enumeration of labeled histories for galled trees" is now out in Algorithms for Molecular Biology!
